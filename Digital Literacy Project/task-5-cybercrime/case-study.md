# Case Study on UPI/ online payment fraud
As mentioned in the heading of this case study, there is going to be a detailed discussion on the UPI or digital finance fraud. UPI(Unified Payments Interface) in a simple definition, is an Indian instant payment system and protocol developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) in 2016. The interface facilitates inter-bank peer-to-peer (P2P) and person-to-merchant (P2M) transactions. It is used on mobile devices to instantly transfer funds between two bank accounts using only a unique UPI ID. It runs as an open source application programming interface (API) on top of the Immediate Payment Service (IMPS), and is regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). Major Indian banks started making their UPI-enabled apps available to customers in August 2016 and the system is today supported by almost all Indian banks. There are also many different methods used by financial criminals to commit UPI fraud. such one method used in these cases is the "Receive Money" scam. To give an exemplified understanding on how an UPI fraud is commited on common masses of India , it has to be explained in a step-to-step format.
- Scammers initiate contact through unsolicited text messages, social media DMs, or dating apps, often with a generic message or a fake job advertisement that promises high pay for little effort.
-  Scammers often project an image of wealth and success (e.g., using pictures of luxury items, fake bank accounts, or rented jets) to convince victims they have the "secret" to wealth.
-  They may offer simple initial tasks (like rating restaurants or liking products online) and actually pay a small amount to establish credibility and make the victim believe the system is legitimate.
-  The scammer introduces a "welfare task" or a fake investment that requires the victim to deposit their own money (often via cryptocurrency) to unlock supposed future earnings or a larger commission. Victims are often directed to a fraudulent website or app that mimics a legitimate platform and shows fabricated "earnings" to encourage further investment.
-  When the victim attempts to withdraw their "money," they are asked to pay more fees, taxes, or a "mirror payment" to release the funds. Once the victim has invested a significant amount of money or starts asking too many questions, the scammer disappears, and the victim is left with financial losses.
